Iteration · minimal · catalog-shaped

Four ways to make § 3 a catalog, not an event page.

Trimmed flat. No participant gallery (decorative for the homepage), no inline charts (jargon for the visitor who hasn't read /research). C1 is pure typography. C2 anchors the right side with the workshop poster, dimmed by default so it doesn't compete with the data — hover lifts it back to full colour. D and E try the catalog metaphor at its most literal: a printed ledger and a timeline rail.

Each option scales to many workshops: old ones shrink to one row; most recent and next get short detail; the structure visibly extends.

What this commits to

Data first, image as backdrop. The poster anchors the right side without grabbing the eye — and rewards a hover with a moment of full-saturation poster art. The Outcome row replaces the cryptic d/ρ stats with one human sentence.

What it gives up

The hover interaction is invisible on touch devices — mobile users see the dimmed state only (the caption signals "hover to lift" but that's a desktop affordance). Mitigation: keep the poster meaningful as atmosphere even without the hover reveal.

D · Ledger The catalog metaphor, literal

A printed ledger, extensible.

Forget the specimen frame; this is just a ledger. Five columns: №, Date, City, Cohort, Format, Status. The most recent row is highlighted in studio pink and carries a one-line italic sub-row of detail. The next row sits below in planning. When workshops 003 and 004 happen, they push the older rows up — and rows from years past collapse to a single line. This scales to twenty workshops without breaking.

What this commits to

Maximum legibility at every workshop count. One workshop reads fine; twenty workshops read fine. Each row is one line of mono and serif — the catalog metaphor is its actual layout. Old workshops just become rows; no special case.

What it gives up

No visual hierarchy — when there are five workshops, none of them get hero treatment. If you ever want a "feature this workshop" moment, the ledger is the wrong shape. Also looks bureaucratic if not paired with strong typography upstream.

E · Timeline rail Time-as-spatial · cadence of a recurring program

A program on a cadence.

A horizontal time rail. The first workshop is a filled dot; the next is an open dot; three faint ghost dots reach out into the future to make the recurrence visible. Below the rail: two compact cards — completed and planned. Older workshops eventually become silent dots in the stretch behind the active card.
